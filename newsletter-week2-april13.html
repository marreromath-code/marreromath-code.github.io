Key Calculus Terms at a Glance

A quick reference table before we dive into the theorems — keep this handy and students can pin down the vocabulary quickly.

TermQuick Definition
ContinuityA function with no holes, jumps, or vertical asymptotes at a point.
Critical PointA point where the derivative is zero or undefined — candidate for extrema.
Inflection PointA point where the concavity of the function changes sign.
AsymptoteA line that the graph of a function approaches but never reaches.
Chain RuleRule for differentiating composite functions: f(g(x))' = f'(g(x)) · g'(x).
Riemann SumAn approximation of the area under a curve using rectangles.
L'Hôpital's RuleA technique for evaluating indeterminate forms (0/0 or ∞/∞).
02 · Theorems · Part I

Important Theorems in Calculus

Part I of our series on the theorems that hold calculus together. Each theorem has a compact statement and a short why it matters note — teach these as stories, not formulas to memorize.

The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus FTC

If f is continuous on [a, b], and F is any antiderivative of f, then:

∫ₐᵇ f(x) dx = F(b) − F(a)
Why it matters: This is the bridge between the two halves of calculus. It tells us that definite integrals (areas) and antiderivatives (accumulation functions) are two sides of the same idea. Solving integrals becomes a matter of running differentiation in reverse.

The Mean Value Theorem MVT

If f is continuous on [a, b] and differentiable on (a, b), then there exists some c in (a, b) such that:

f ′(c) = (f(b) − f(a)) / (b − a)
Intuition: At some point, the instantaneous rate of change (slope of the tangent line) equals the average rate of change (slope of the secant line between the endpoints). If you drove from A to B at an average of 60 mph, at some moment your speedometer really read 60.

Rolle's Theorem Special case of MVT

If f is continuous on [a, b], differentiable on (a, b), and f(a) = f(b), then there exists some c in (a, b) with:

f ′(c) = 0
Intuition: If a smooth function starts and ends at the same height, it must have a "flat spot" (a horizontal tangent) somewhere in between. Rolle's is the special case of the MVT with equal endpoints — it helped pave the way to the MVT itself.

03 · Prerequisites · Part I

What Students Need Before Calculus

Calculus builds directly on Algebra, Geometry, and Pre-Calculus. Students who struggle often have rust on those skills — not the calculus itself. Here's the list I send to students the summer before they take the course.

➕ Algebra Essentials

  • Solving linear and quadratic equations
  • Factoring polynomials (difference of squares, trinomials)
  • Rational expressions: simplifying, adding, multiplying
  • Laws of exponents and radicals
  • Completing the square
  • Inequalities and interval notation
  • Absolute-value equations and inequalities

📈 Functions & Graphs

  • Definition of a function, domain & range
  • Function notation: f(g(x)), composite and inverse
  • Transformations: shifts, stretches, reflections
  • Linear, quadratic, and piecewise functions
  • Reading and graphing from equations
04 · Prerequisites · Part II

Trigonometry & Pre-Calculus Concepts

📐 Trigonometry

  • Unit-circle angles in radians and degrees
  • Values of sin, cos, tan at key angles (π/6, π/4, π/3, π/2)
  • Pythagorean identity: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
  • Reciprocal identities: csc, sec, cot
  • Graphs of sin, cos, and their transformations
  • Inverse trig functions (arcsin, arccos, arctan)
  • Sum and double-angle formulas

🌀 Pre-Calculus Concepts

  • Polynomial and rational functions, end behavior
  • Exponential and logarithmic functions, properties
  • Parametric equations (optional, but helpful)
  • Sequences and series — arithmetic & geometric
  • Limits (intuitive understanding before formal)
  • Piecewise-defined functions
Tip: Before the first exam, have students self-assess against this list. They often discover the gap isn't calculus — it's factoring.
