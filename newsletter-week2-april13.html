Key Calculus Definitions

Foundations
The Limit
limx→c f(x) = L

We say the limit of f(x) as x approaches c equals L if f(x) can be made arbitrarily close to L by taking x sufficiently close to c (but not equal to c). It is the cornerstone concept of all Calculus.

The Derivative
f'(x) = limh→0 [f(x+h)−f(x)] / h

The derivative measures the instantaneous rate of change of a function. Geometrically, it represents the slope of the tangent line to the graph of f at the point x.

Continuity
limx→c f(x) = f(c)

A function f is continuous at x = c if: (1) f(c) is defined, (2) the limit as x→c exists, and (3) the limit equals f(c). The graph has no holes, jumps, or breaks at c.

The Definite Integral
∫ab f(x) dx

The definite integral of f from a to b is the limit of Riemann sums — the net signed area between the graph of f and the x-axis over [a, b].

Differentiability
f'(c) exists

A function is differentiable at x = c if its derivative exists there. Differentiability implies continuity, but continuity alone does not guarantee differentiability (e.g., |x| at x = 0).

Antiderivative (Indefinite Integral)
∫ f(x) dx = F(x) + C

F is an antiderivative of f if F'(x) = f(x). The indefinite integral represents the family of all antiderivatives, differing only by a constant C.

💡 Remember: Limits, derivatives, and integrals are deeply connected. The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus links derivatives and integrals in one of the most beautiful relationships in all of mathematics.

Important Theorems in Calculus

Core Results
The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus FTC
Part I: If f is continuous on [a, b] and F(x) = ∫ₐˣ f(t) dt, then F'(x) = f(x).
Part II: If F is any antiderivative of f, then ∫ₐᵇ f(x) dx = F(b) − F(a).
Why it matters: The grand unifying theorem of Calculus — differentiation and integration are inverse operations.
The Mean Value Theorem MVT
If f is continuous on [a, b] and differentiable on (a, b), then ∃ c ∈ (a, b) such that f'(c) = [ f(b) − f(a) ] / (b − a).
Intuition: At some point, the instantaneous rate of change equals the average rate of change over the interval.
Rolle's Theorem Special Case of MVT
If f is continuous on [a, b], differentiable on (a, b), and f(a) = f(b), then ∃ c ∈ (a, b) such that f'(c) = 0.
Intuition: If a smooth function starts and ends at the same height, it must have a horizontal tangent somewhere in between.
The Intermediate Value Theorem IVT
If f is continuous on [a, b] and k is any value strictly between f(a) and f(b), then ∃ c ∈ (a, b) such that f(c) = k.
Application: Guarantees that a root exists in an interval when the function changes sign.
The Extreme Value Theorem EVT
If f is continuous on a closed interval [a, b], then f attains both an absolute maximum and an absolute minimum on [a, b].
Key insight: Guarantees optimal values exist — the foundation of all optimization problems in Calculus.
L'Hôpital's Rule Limits
If lim f(x)/g(x) gives 0/0 or ∞/∞, then lim f(x)/g(x) = lim f'(x)/g'(x), provided the latter limit exists.
Warning: Only applies to indeterminate forms. Always verify the form before applying.

  • Solving linear and quadratic equations
  • Factoring polynomials
  • Rational expressions
  • Laws of exponents and radicals
  • Completing the square
  • Inequalities and interval notation
  • Absolute value equations

  • Domain and range of a function
  • Function notation: f(x), g(t)

  • Composite functions: f(g(x))

  • The natural base e and ln(x)
  • Limits — intuitive understanding
  • Sequences and series (intro)

  • Slope formula and equations of lines
  • Distance and midpoint formulas

  • Mathematical notation fluency

Featured Science News: Artemis II

Space Exploration
🔴 Breaking — April 2026

Artemis II: Humanity Returns to the Moon — Historic Mission Complete!

NASA's Artemis II — the first crewed mission beyond low Earth orbit in over 50 years — returned to Earth after a breathtaking 7-hour lunar flyby. The crew broke Apollo 13's 1970 distance record, reaching 252,756 miles from Earth — the farthest humans have ever traveled. They witnessed Earthset, Earthrise, and a solar eclipse from the Orion spacecraft.
