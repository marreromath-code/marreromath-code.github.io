02 · Quick Hits

Two Stories Worth Reading This Week

🩺 Medical Breakthrough

Researchers have developed a new oxygen-delivering gel to treat chronic wounds by locally reversing hypoxia — a condition where low oxygen prevents healing in conditions like diabetic ulcers. Early results suggest faster healing with minimal systemic effects.

🌍 Climate Alert

New data confirm Greenland's ice sheet is melting at an accelerated rate, raising sea-level-rise projections. The story connects directly to exponential decay models and rates-of-change conversations in Algebra I and Calculus.

03 · AI & Math Learning

How the Next Generation Tackles Math

A global survey from the Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics (SIAM) reveals a massive shift in how students learn math. Nearly 70% of top-performing students in the U.S. and U.K. now use AI as a primary learning tool.

The teacher's nuance

While ~40% of respondents say AI reduces "math anxiety," 62% of educators interviewed still emphasize that consistent, deliberate practice remains the gold standard for real mastery. AI is the amplifier — it does not replace the reps.

04 · Math News

The "Nobel" of Math: Gerd Faltings Wins the 2026 Abel Prize

Mathematician Gerd Faltings has been awarded the 2026 Abel Prize for his decades of work in arithmetic geometry — including the theorem now bearing his name (formerly the Mordell conjecture), which helped pave the way toward the modern proof of Fermat's Last Theorem.

The Abel Prize is often referred to as the Nobel Prize of mathematics. It's a wonderful classroom hook for talking about why number theory matters, how conjectures become theorems, and how one result can ripple across a whole field.

05 · Science · Origins & Innovations

A 500-Million-Year-Old Spider Ancestor & A Return to the Moon

🕷️ The Spider's Ancestor Uncovered

Paleontologists at Harvard have identified Megachelicerax cousteaui, a ~500-million-year-old fossil that pushes back the origin of chelicerates (spiders and scorpions) by 20 million years. The specimen features a pincer-like "chelicera," proving that the anatomical blueprint for modern arachnids was established far earlier than previously thought.

🌕 Record-Breaking Lunar Flyby

A new crewed mission has completed a record-breaking lunar flyby — the first crewed flight to the vicinity of the Moon in over 50 years. This paves the way for a permanent human presence on the lunar surface, and rekindles the kind of numerical-precision engineering that built entire branches of modern mathematics.
