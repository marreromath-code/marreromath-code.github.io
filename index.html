About Dr. Marrero

Dr. Carlos Marrero, Ed.D., ES, MS

Mathematics Educator & Faculty Professor · Grades 6–12 & Post-Secondary

Dr. Carlos Marrero is a mathematics educator with over 21 years of demonstrated excellence at the secondary and post-secondary levels, currently serving as a Math Adjunct with Georgia Virtual Learning (Georgia Department of Education).

His teaching philosophy is rooted in the idealism of Plato — who considered mathematics the only universal constant — and the belief that a teacher's role is to act as a transformer, one who triggers students' drive to explore, question, and solve critical problems. His instructional approach is grounded in three pillars: discussion, analysis, and synthesis, with a strong emphasis on building connectivity across mathematical fields.

Dr. Marrero holds a Doctor of Education (Ed.D.) from Nova Southeastern University, where his dissertation focused on implementing technology-enhanced instruction in Algebra I to increase student academic achievement. He also holds an Educational Specialist (ES) and Master of Science (MS) in Mathematics Education, and a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics Education & Computer Programming from Havana University.

Beyond the classroom, he has served as an instructional coach, department chair, curriculum specialist, and manuscript reviewer for NCTM's Mathematics Teacher journal. He is a member of NCTM, the American Mathematical Association, and the Honor Society of Phi Kappa Phi.
