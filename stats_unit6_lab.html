σ MAD & Standard Deviation
Both measure how spread out data is from the mean. Lower = more consistent. SD is more powerful because it squares differences.
MAD — 3 Steps
1
Find the mean of the data
2
Find |x − mean| for each value (absolute difference)
3
Find the mean of those differences
Standard Deviation σ — 5 Steps
1
Find the mean
2
Find (x − mean) for each value
3
Square each difference: (x − mean)²
4
Find the mean of the squares
5
√ the result (square-root)

⚠️ Outliers
An extreme value much less or greater than the rest. Use the IQR Fence Method to test any value mathematically.

Q1 − 1.5(IQR)

Upper Fence (UF)
Q3 + 1.5(IQR)

🔗 Correlation & Causation
r is between −1 and 1. Four traits: Form, Direction, Strength, Outliers. Correlation ≠ Causation.
The Four Traits of Correlation
1. Form
Type of relationship the data creates: Linear, Quadratic, Exponential, or None
2. Direction
Positive (rises) or Negative (falls left→right)
3. Strength
How closely points follow the pattern: Strong / Moderate / Weak / None
4. Outliers
Points that do NOT fit the pattern of the rest of the data

📈 Regression & Predictions
Linear regression finds the best-fit equation. Interpolation = predicting inside the data range (reliable). Extrapolation = outside the range (less reliable).
Interpolation
Predicting a value INSIDE the data range. More reliable.
Extrapolation
Predicting a value OUTSIDE the data range. Less reliable!

Linear vs. Exponential vs. Quadratic — Choosing the Best Model
📈 National Debt → Exponential 🎟️ Ticket Prices → Quadratic
Rule: The model with |r| closest to 1 is the best fit. Linear: y = ax+b · Exponential: y = a(b)ˣ · Quadratic: y = ax²+bx+c

When Outliers Exist — Which Stat to Use
For Center → Use Median
Median is the "resistant" center. Mean gets pulled toward the outlier and misrepresents the data.
For Spread → Use IQR
IQR measures the middle 50% and ignores extreme values. Range and SD are both affected.
